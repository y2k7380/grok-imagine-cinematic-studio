# 🎬 PRODUCTION BIBLE — 공무도하가 (Gongmudoha-ga)
**Project:** 공무도하가 | The Uncrossed River  
**Version:** v3.4 Full Cinematic Studio  
**Logline:** In the mists of an ancient river, a wife's desperate song cannot stop fate. A timeless Korean lament of love, warning, and irreversible loss.  
**Target Runtime:** 55–70 seconds (6 clips, mostly 6–10s)  
**Format:** 16:9 cinematic, 720p, real video clips assembled with ffmpeg stream copy  
**Creative Direction (Locked):** "The River Remembers" — Poetic Historical Realism. Hyper-detailed, emotionally raw, slow-cinema influenced tragic short. The river is the antagonist. Wind and water are characters. Single heroine with devastating micro-performance. Authentic Gojoseon/early Goguryeo world from murals and history.

## LOCKED VARIABLES (verbatim in every prompt)

[HER: 24-year-old woman of ancient Gojoseon/early Goguryeo, timeless Korean beauty. High cheekbones, almond-shaped deep brown eyes holding oceans of sorrow and love, full lips, thick dark hair worn long and loose with a single simple cloth ribbon that the wind constantly plays with. Fair skin flushed from wind and grief, subtle wind-chapped cheeks. Slender yet strong body from riverside life, graceful and natural movements. Costume: authentic Goguryeo-inspired hanbok reconstructed from ancient tomb murals — off-white/cream jeogori with wide flowing sleeves and subtle earth-tone embroidery at cuffs and hem, long high-waisted flowing white chima skirt that billows dramatically in wind, thin brown woven sash tied in simple knot at waist, no jewelry except a small hand-woven bracelet on one wrist. Bare feet or simple cloth shoes on cold wet stones. Base presence: quiet dignity cracking into desperate plea, then raw grief and hollow resignation.]

[HUSBAND: Broad-shouldered man in his late 20s, seen mostly as silhouette or back/side in heavy mist. Simple dark earth-tone working clothes, headcloth, sturdy. Determined but ultimately doomed stride. Brief and symbolic only — never compete with [HER].]

[RIVER: The fatal river — wide, swift-flowing, steel-gray and cold blue-green water with white foam and spray on dark jagged rocks. Heavy atmospheric mist and fog that swallows the far bank and low mountains. Cold, indifferent, alive as antagonist. Banks: wet dark rocks, sparse tall reeds, pebbles. Constant subtle movement even in stills.]

[ERA_WORLD: Late Gojoseon to early Goguryeo period, ~2nd–1st century BCE. Sparse, humble thatched structures visible only distantly if at all. No anachronisms. Natural world completely dominant: wind, water, fog, low horizon light, reeds. Human elements minimal and period-accurate.]

[COLOR_LIGHT_PALETTE: Desaturated cinematic realism — dominant cold slate blues, steel grays, muted olive-browns, soft fog whites. One persistent subtle warm accent thread (soft amber-gold on [HER]'s inner collar or sash lining, or low sun on water surface). Lighting: soft overcast dawn or blue-hour dusk with gentle volumetric rays and god rays piercing the mist. High emotional contrast on face and eyes. Subtle film grain, natural halation, anamorphic character (gentle horizontal flares possible on water highlights).]

[STYLE_DNA: Cinematic historical poetic realism. Anamorphic 35mm/50mm lens personality (subtle flares, compressed backgrounds, beautiful bokeh in mist). Camera: slow tracking parallel to river, gentle push-ins on face and hands, occasional static Ozu-inspired holds for pure grief, wind as constant motivator of fabric and hair. Ultra-detailed authentic textures (fabric weave, wet stone, skin, hair strands). Emotional truth above all. No modern polish, no fantasy elements.]

[POEM_CORE: The four lines of the ancient "공무도하가" (Gongmudoha-ga), the oldest surviving Korean lyric poem.  
1. 공무도하 (Gong mu do ha) — Sir, do not cross the river.  
2. 공경도하 (Gong gyeong do ha) — Sir, yet you crossed the river.  
3. 타하이사 (Ta ha i sa) — You fell in and died.  
4. 당내공하 (Dang nae gong ha) — Alas, what am I to do with you?  
Sung by a single raw female voice with traditional Korean folk delivery — clear, breaking with emotion on repeats, increasing desperation and then hollow finality. Minimal reverb. Diegetic river and wind as primary "orchestra".]

[VISUAL_MOTIFS: Wind constantly moving hair and wide sleeves (life force and fate). River flow as unstoppable time/death. [HER]'s reaching hand. The ribbon in hair as fragile connection. Mist swallowing figures. Recurring profile or three-quarter view of [HER] looking toward the river.]

**Problem reported:** Baked audio in initial clips contained mixed English + Japanese vocals/singing instead of pure Korean or ambient. This makes the film unusable for a Korean-language project.

**Root cause:** 
- Current image_to_video tool bakes audio based on the English motion prompt.
- Even with "Korean folk singing" in the spec, the model often generates plausible but wrong-language or mixed vocals (English/Japanese common in training data).
- Prompts were not strong enough with explicit bans and exact lyrics repeated.

**Fix applied in this production:**
- All clips have *_silent.mp4 versions created with `ffmpeg -c:v copy -an`.
- New clean final: `clips/final_gongmudohaga_silent.mp4` (visual only, no bad audio).
