**Genre:** Dark Comedy / Neo-Noir Crime  
**Director Signature:** Guy Ritchie meets the Coen Brothers (stylized violence, sharp dialogue, ironic detachment)  
**Target Duration:** 10–15 minutes  
**Tone:** Stylish, cynical, darkly funny, morally gray  
**Key Themes:** Professional pride vs. personal burnout, the absurdity of violence, loyalty in a disposable world

## Character DNA

**Victor "The Cleaner" Malone (Protagonist – Late 40s)**  
- Professional hitman nearing retirement  
- Meticulous, proud of his "craft," deeply superstitious about "one last job"  
- Signature trait: Talks to his targets politely before killing them

**Frankie "The Kid" (Young rival – Mid 20s)**  
- Flashy, sloppy, social media-obsessed new generation of killer  
- Represents everything Victor despises about the modern world  
- Wants to "inherit" Victor's reputation

**Supporting:**  
- Victor's long-suffering handler (wants him to retire)  
- The Target who turns out to be more complicated than expected

---

## Key Visual & Thematic Motifs

- **Professional Pride:** Victor treats killing like fine craftsmanship — tools are cleaned, rituals are followed
- **Ironic Violence:** Stylized action that feels almost comedic in its precision
- **Digital vs. Analog:** Victor uses old-school methods; Frankie uses apps, drones, and social media
- **The "Last Job" Trope:** Subverted — Victor keeps finding reasons why *this* one isn't the last

---

## Recommended Production Approach

**Phase 1 – Pre-Production**  
- **Narrative Arc Strategist** and **Sequence Director** map the ironic structure and tonal balance  
- **Stunt & Action Choreographer** designs stylized, almost theatrical violence  
- **Imagine Prompt Master** helps craft sharp, quotable dialogue

**Phase 2 – Core Production**  
- Fast but controlled shooting pace with heavy coverage for comedic timing  
- **Sequence Director** ensures action sequences have clear, ironic punctuation  
- Sound design balances stylish score with awkward, realistic foley for comedic contrast

**Phase 3 – Post & Polish**  
- **Quality Assurance Guardian** protects tonal consistency — never lets it become purely comedic or purely dark  
- Editing focuses on ironic juxtaposition and precise comedic timing  
- Color grade enhances the slightly sickly, stylized neo-noir look

---

## Notes for Key Agents

**Narrative Arc Strategist + Sequence Director:**  
The humor comes from professionalism meeting absurdity. Victor's pride is both his strength and his curse.

**Stunt & Action Choreographer:**  
Violence should be stylish but never glorified. The precision itself is the joke.

**Quality Assurance Guardian:**  
Guard the tonal line carefully. Too silly and it loses weight. Too dark and the comedy dies.
