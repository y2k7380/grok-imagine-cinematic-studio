# Production Bible: Shadow Protocol

**Genre:** Neo-Noir Thriller  
**Director Signature:** David Fincher  
**Runtime:** 128 minutes  
**Aspect Ratio:** 2.39:1  
**Color Palette:** Desaturated greens, deep reds, clinical whites, heavy shadows

## Logline
A brilliant but broken FBI profiler is recruited to catch a killer who is recreating famous unsolved murders — only to discover the killer is using her own past as the blueprint.

## Synopsis
Agent Lena Voss is pulled out of retirement when a new serial killer begins perfectly replicating cold cases she failed to solve 15 years ago. As the body count rises, Lena realizes the killer knows intimate details of her life — including the murder of her sister that was never solved.

## Main Characters

### Agent Lena Voss
- **Age:** 41  
- **Visual:** Sharp cheekbones, tired eyes, always in black turtlenecks  
- **Personality:** Analytical, emotionally detached, secretly terrified  
- **Arc:** From cold professional to confronting her deepest trauma

### The Copycat (Antagonist)
- **Visual:** Never fully shown until final act  
- **Signature:** Leaves perfectly framed crime scene photos

## Visual Style
- **Lighting:** Low-key, heavy use of practical shadows and rain  
- **Camera:** Precise, clinical framing. Lots of Dutch angles and reflections  
- **Color Treatment:** Cold, desaturated with occasional violent red accents

## Tone & Themes
**Tone:** Cold, clinical, psychologically suffocating  
**Themes:** Memory vs reality, the cost of obsession, identity

## Key Sequences
1. **Opening** — Rainy night murder recreated perfectly  
2. **Inciting Incident** — Lena receives the first photo  
3. **Midpoint** — She realizes the killer was in the room with her sister  
4. **Climax** — Final confrontation in the original crime scene

## Director Notes (Fincher Style)
- Every frame must feel intentional  
- Use practical locations and lighting  
- Sound design should be hyper-realistic  
- Let silence and tension do the heavy lifting
