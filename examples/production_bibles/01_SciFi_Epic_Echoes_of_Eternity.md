# Production Bible: Echoes of Eternity

**Genre:** Sci-Fi Epic  
**Director Signature:** Denis Villeneuve  
**Runtime:** 142 minutes  
**Aspect Ratio:** 2.39:1  
**Color Palette:** Desaturated teal, warm amber, deep space black

## Logline
A grieving astrophysicist discovers that time itself is a living organism and must decide whether to save her dying daughter or preserve the future of humanity.

## Synopsis
In 2147, Dr. Elara Voss discovers an ancient alien signal that reveals time is not linear but a conscious entity. As her daughter’s terminal illness accelerates, Elara must navigate a collapsing timeline while confronting the moral cost of altering destiny.

## Main Characters

### Dr. Elara Voss (Lead)
- **Age:** 38  
- **Visual:** Tall, sharp features, silver-streaked hair, always wearing a worn NASA flight jacket  
- **Personality:** Brilliant, emotionally guarded, quietly desperate  
- **Arc:** From detached scientist to mother willing to sacrifice everything

### Captain Kael Ren (Co-Lead)
- **Age:** 42  
- **Visual:** Weathered, intense eyes, cybernetic arm  
- **Personality:** Loyal, haunted by past missions  
- **Arc:** Learns to feel again through Elara’s love

## Visual Style
- **Lighting:** Natural harsh sunlight mixed with cold fluorescent ship interiors  
- **Camera:** Slow, deliberate movements. Long takes. Extreme close-ups on eyes and hands  
- **Color Treatment:** Heavy teal/orange cinematic grade with crushed blacks

## Tone & Themes
**Tone:** Somber, majestic, emotionally devastating  
**Themes:** Time as love, sacrifice vs destiny, what makes us human

## Key Sequences (8-Beat Structure)
1. **Opening Image** — Elara watching her daughter sleep under alien stars  
2. **Inciting Incident** — Discovery of the Time Signal  
3. **First Turning Point** — Daughter’s condition worsens dramatically  
4. **Midpoint** — Elara learns she can change the past  
5. **Dark Night** — She alters the timeline and loses Kael  
6. **Second Turning Point** — She realizes she must let go  
7. **Climax** — Final confrontation with Time itself  
8. **Resolution** — Emotional, quiet ending with hope

## Director Notes (Villeneuve Style)
- Silence is as important as dialogue  
- Let the audience feel the weight of time  
- Use practical effects wherever possible  
- Score should feel ancient and futuristic simultaneously
