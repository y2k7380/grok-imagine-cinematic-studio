# Production Bible: The Forgotten Crown

**Genre:** Fantasy Adventure  
**Director Signature:** Ridley Scott  
**Runtime:** 148 minutes  
**Aspect Ratio:** 2.39:1  
**Color Palette:** Rich earth tones, deep forest greens, mystical golds, blood reds

## Logline
A disgraced knight discovers he is the last descendant of an ancient bloodline and must reclaim a lost crown before a dark sorcerer uses it to plunge the world into eternal night.

## Synopsis
Sir Thorne Blackwood, once the greatest knight in the kingdom, now lives as a drunk in the ruins of his family castle. When a mysterious woman arrives with news that the long-lost Crown of Eldrath has resurfaced, Thorne must overcome his shame and lead a ragtag group on a quest across dangerous lands.

## Main Characters

### Sir Thorne Blackwood
- **Age:** 39  
- **Visual:** Scarred, muscular, haunted expression  
- **Personality:** Bitter, honorable, secretly noble  
- **Arc:** From broken man to legendary hero

### Lady Elowen (Mystic Guide)
- **Visual:** Ethereal, silver hair, glowing runes on skin  
- **Personality:** Wise, mysterious, compassionate

## Visual Style
- **Lighting:** Dramatic god rays through ancient forests, torch-lit caves  
- **Camera:** Epic wide shots mixed with intimate character moments  
- **Color Treatment:** Rich, painterly, with strong contrast

## Tone & Themes
**Tone:** Grand, mythic, emotionally grounded  
**Themes:** Redemption, legacy, the cost of power

## Key Sequences
1. **Opening** — Thorne drinking alone in ruined hall  
2. **Inciting Incident** — Arrival of Lady Elowen  
3. **Midpoint** — Discovery that the crown is inside the enemy fortress  
4. **Climax** — Epic final battle at the edge of the world

## Director Notes (Scott Style)
- Make the world feel ancient and lived-in  
- Balance spectacle with quiet character moments  
- Use practical locations and sets  
- Score should feel both medieval and timeless
