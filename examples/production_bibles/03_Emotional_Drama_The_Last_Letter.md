# Production Bible: The Last Letter

**Genre:** Emotional Drama  
**Director Signature:** Greta Gerwig  
**Runtime:** 118 minutes  
**Aspect Ratio:** 1.85:1  
**Color Palette:** Warm pastels, golden hour tones, soft film grain

## Logline
A young woman discovers a box of unsent love letters from her late grandmother and embarks on a journey to deliver them — uncovering family secrets and her own capacity for love.

## Synopsis
After her grandmother’s death, Maya finds 47 unsent love letters written to a man she never mentioned. Determined to find him, Maya travels across the country, meeting people who knew her grandmother and slowly understanding the woman she thought she knew.

## Main Characters

### Maya Chen
- **Age:** 27  
- **Visual:** Warm smile, expressive eyes, casual style  
- **Personality:** Curious, empathetic, slightly lost  
- **Arc:** From running from commitment to embracing vulnerability

### Grandma (in flashbacks)
- **Visual:** Vibrant, rebellious, full of life  
- **Personality:** Fierce, romantic, independent

## Visual Style
- **Lighting:** Soft natural light, golden hour emphasis  
- **Camera:** Handheld intimacy mixed with sweeping landscapes  
- **Color Treatment:** Warm, nostalgic film look with gentle saturation

## Tone & Themes
**Tone:** Tender, hopeful, emotionally resonant  
**Themes:** Legacy of love, courage to feel, intergenerational healing

## Key Sequences
1. **Opening** — Maya cleaning out grandmother’s attic  
2. **Inciting Incident** — Discovery of the letters  
3. **Midpoint** — Meeting the man from the letters  
4. **Climax** — Maya reads the final unsent letter aloud

## Director Notes (Gerwig Style)
- Let conversations feel natural and overlapping  
- Use music to carry emotional weight  
- Focus on small, human moments  
- End on a note of quiet hope
